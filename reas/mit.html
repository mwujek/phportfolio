Education - MIT

Reas, a graduate student and researcher at the MIT, worked in the Aesthetics and Computation Group within the MIT Media Lab from 1999 to 2001. His research was developed with colleague Ben Fry. The MIT Press has published two works by Reas. The book, 10 PRINT CHR$(205.5+RND(1)); : GOTO 10, takes a single line of code and uses it as a lens through which to consider the phenomenon of creative computing and the way computer programs exist in culture. The other book (more similar to a manual), A Programming Handbook for Visual Designers and Artists, serves as a practical guide and reference for the Processing application. This book promotes digital literacy for both beginners and expert programmmers.
