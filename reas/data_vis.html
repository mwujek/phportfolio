Information Visualization & Data Mining

Information visualization is a process which harnesses the immense power of visual communication in order to explain, in an understandable manner, the relationships of meaning, cause and dependency which can be found among the great abstract masses of information generated by scientific and social processes. By visualizing sets of data, individuals are able to make unique associations and connections that they may have not reached before viewing the "visualized" data.

In a world with countless interconnected networks that send and receive immense amounts of information, data is growing faster than ever. We are in the age of "big data". This means there is more data available than ever before and there are more gets to access that data as well. Unlike printed infographics, interactive visualizations can be explored as users interact with the display. Not only are these these graphics highly informative, but they also demonstrative artistic qualities. Interactive designs create very interesting possibilities for the future of design and our understanding of statistics and information.

Reas' work with Processing has made data visualization much more accessible to artists and designers with limited coding experience.
